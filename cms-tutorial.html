Introduction & What the CMS Controls

The CaltransBizConnect Content Management System (CMS) is a web-based editor at /admin-cms.html. It lets authorized administrators update the text, images, and structured content shown on the public-facing website — without touching any code.

What Can You Edit with the CMS?

ModuleWhat It Controls
PagesHero text, page sections, component content, page title, and meta description
FAQ ManagerAll questions and answers on the FAQ page and FAQ accordion sections
Global SettingsSite-wide values: site title, contact email, phone number, social links, logo URL
Media LibraryAll images used in CMS-editable sections; upload, manage, and delete

What the CMS Does NOT Control

  • The site navigation menu links
  • User account dashboard pages
  • Form functionality and validation
  • The opportunities listing (live database content)
  • Application processing workflows
  • CSS styling and visual design (requires a developer)
ℹ️
Platform administration is separate

To manage user accounts, approve opportunities, or view analytics, see the Platform Admin Guide. The CMS only handles website content.

Logging In

How to Access the CMS

1
Open your browser and navigate to: caltransbizconnect.org/admin-cms.html
2
The CMS login screen loads with two fields: Email and Password.
3
Enter the email address associated with your admin account. This is the same email you use to log into the main platform.
4
Enter your CMS password. Depending on how your account was set up, this may be your platform password or a separate CMS-specific password.
5
Click Sign In. If your credentials are correct, the CMS dashboard loads within a few seconds.
⚠️
Session duration is 24 hours

After 24 hours, your CMS session expires and you must sign in again. Always save your work before leaving the CMS — unsaved changes are lost when your session expires.

If Your Login Fails

  • Double-check for typos in your email address — it must match exactly.
  • Check that Caps Lock is not on when entering your password.
  • If you recently changed your password via the CMS "Change Password" feature, use the new password.
  • If still unable to log in, contact the platform administrator to verify your account has CMS access.

How to Log Out

1
Locate the Logout button in the CMS sidebar (left column, near the bottom).
2
Click it. Your session token is invalidated immediately and you are returned to the login screen. Always log out when finished, especially on shared computers.

Pages Management

The Pages module is where you edit the content of all public-facing website pages. Click Pages in the sidebar to open it.

Reading the Page List

ColumnDescription
Page TitleThe human-readable title shown in the editor
Last UpdatedDate and time of the most recent save
System PageBadge shown for built-in pages that cannot be deleted
ActionsEdit, View (live link), and Delete (custom pages only)

System pages appear first in fixed order: Home, For Small Businesses, For Prime Contractors, Opportunities, How It Works, Resources, FAQ, Support Services, Eligibility, Contact. Custom pages appear after these, alphabetically.

How to Edit a Page

1
In the Pages list, find the page you want and click its Edit button.
2
The Page Editor opens with two main areas: Page Metadata at the top (Title, Meta Description) and Page Sections below.
3
Edit the Page Title if needed. This is shown in the browser tab and search results. Edit the Meta Description to update the Google snippet — keep it under 160 characters.
4
To edit a section's content, click the Edit button on that section's row. The Section Editor opens in-line with all fields for that component type.
5
Make your changes and click Save at the top of the page editor. Changes are saved to the database and appear live on the website immediately.
🚫
No auto-save

The CMS does not auto-save. If you navigate away without clicking Save, all your changes will be lost. Save frequently, especially after significant edits.

How to Reorder Page Sections

1
Locate the section you want to move in the section list.
2
Click the up arrow to move it one position up, or the down arrow to move it one position down. Repeat until the section is in the desired position.
3
Click Save to preserve the new order.

How to Add a New Section

1
In the Page Editor, scroll to the bottom of the section list and click + Add New Section. A dropdown appears listing all available component types.
2
Select the component type that best fits the content you want to add (see Section 5 for the full reference).
3
A new empty section of that type is added. Click Edit on the new section to fill in its content, then Save.

How to Delete a Section

1
In the Page Editor, click the Delete Section (trash) icon on the section's row.
2
Confirm in the dialog. Then click Save. The section is permanently removed from that page.
⚠️
No undo for deletions

Once you save after deleting a section, the content is gone permanently. Before deleting, copy the content to a text document if there is any chance you'll need to restore it.

How to Create a New Custom Page

1
In the Pages list, click + New Page.
2
Enter a Page Title and a Page Slug. The slug becomes the URL — for example, slug vendor-resources creates the page at /vendor-resources.html. Use only lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens.
3
Click Create Page. The new page appears in the Pages list. Click Edit to start adding sections.
💡
Custom pages are not automatically linked

Creating a page in the CMS does not add it to the site navigation. To make a custom page accessible, link to it from another page's content or ask a developer to add it to the nav.

Section Types Reference

When you add or edit a page section, the editing interface changes based on the component type. Below is a complete reference for every available type.

text Single-line Text

A plain text input for short content like titles, names, or captions. No formatting allowed. Use for headings or brief labels.

textarea Multi-line Text

A larger text area for paragraphs. Basic HTML tags (bold, italic, links) are supported. Use for body copy, descriptions, or summaries.

url URL Field

A validated text input for web addresses. Always include https://. Used for button links, image source URLs, or external references.

select Dropdown

A predefined list of options. Common uses: selecting a layout variant, a color theme, or content alignment. Available options are fixed by the developer.

checkbox Toggle

A boolean on/off switch. Use to show or hide a section, enable a feature, or control visibility of an element.

media Image Picker

Opens the Media Library to select an uploaded image. Always set meaningful alt text for accessibility compliance.

button CTA Button

Configures a call-to-action button: button text, destination URL, and style (primary/secondary). Use for prominent action links within sections.

card-list Card Grid

A repeating list of cards, each with a title, body text, optional image, and optional button. Use for feature highlights, program overviews, or resource listings.

steps-list Numbered Steps

A numbered process list where each step has a short label and detail text. Use for how-it-works sequences, onboarding flows, or application processes.

tile-list Tile Grid

A compact grid of tiles, each with a title, short description, and optional link. Use for quick-link tiles or icon-based navigation sections.

accordion-list Expandable FAQ

Collapsible question/answer pairs (accordion). Each item has a question (always visible) and an answer (shown when expanded). Use for inline FAQs or help content within a page.

cta-list CTA Sections

One or more full call-to-action sections, each with a headline, supporting text, and a button. Use for key program actions at the bottom of or between major sections.

string-list Simple List

A plain bullet list of text strings with no sub-fields. Use for eligibility requirements, feature lists, or quick facts that don't need individual titles.

pathway-card Pathway Card

A specialized card for "pathway" navigation used on the Home page to direct users to Small Business or Prime Contractor sections. Has a fixed structure defined by the developer.

Working with List-Type Sections

For any component type with "list" in its name, the editor provides a list of items you manage individually:

ActionHow to Do It
Add a new itemClick + Add Item below the item list. A new empty row is created at the bottom.
Edit an itemClick the Edit (pencil) icon on the item row. Fields expand inline for editing.
Reorder itemsUse the up/down arrow buttons on each item row to change its position.
Delete an itemClick the Delete (trash) icon and confirm. Save the page to finalize.

Rich Text Editor

Some fields — particularly long text answers in FAQs, accordion answers, and description fields — display a formatting toolbar above the text area. This is the Rich Text Editor.

Toolbar Buttons

ButtonWhat It DoesWhen to Use
B BoldWraps selected text in <strong> tags to make it bold on the page.Highlight key terms, requirements, or important phrases. Use sparingly.
I ItalicWraps selected text in <em> tags to italicize it.Document titles, technical terms, or gentle emphasis. Use sparingly.
• ListInserts an unordered (bullet) list at the cursor position.Eligibility criteria, document checklists, feature lists.
🔗 LinkInserts an anchor tag at the cursor. You'll be prompted for the URL and link text.References to external resources, other pages, or downloadable forms.

Character Count

Fields with a maximum length display a live character counter below the text area (e.g., "143 / 160 characters"). When you exceed the limit, the counter turns red. This most commonly applies to meta description fields — keep them under 160 characters for best search results.

Tips for Rich Text Fields

  • Select first, then click the toolbar button — highlight text before clicking Bold or Italic.
  • Don't paste formatted content from Word or Google Docs — it may carry hidden formatting that breaks the layout. Paste as plain text first (Ctrl+Shift+V on most systems), then format using the toolbar.
  • Test all links before saving — paste the URL into a new browser tab to confirm it works before embedding it.
  • Use lists for three or more items — if you have two or fewer items, write them as inline text instead.

FAQ Manager

The FAQ Manager controls all questions and answers displayed on the FAQ page and FAQ accordion sections across the site. Click FAQ Manager in the sidebar to open it.

Viewing and Filtering FAQs

  • Category filter dropdown — show FAQs from one category only. Select "All Categories" to see everything.
  • Search field — filter by keywords in the question text. Results update as you type.

The table displays: Category badge, Question (first 50 characters), Answer snippet (first 100 characters), Status (Active/Inactive), and action buttons.

How to Create a New FAQ

1
Click + New FAQ above the table. The FAQ editor modal opens.
2
Fill in the Category field. Select an existing category from the dropdown or type a new name. Categories group FAQs on the FAQ page under headings.
3
Type the Question as a complete sentence (e.g., "How do I submit my capability statement?").
4
Type the Answer. Use the Rich Text Editor toolbar if formatting is needed. Keep answers concise and direct.
5
Set the Status dropdown to Active to make the FAQ visible on the site, or Inactive to save as a draft.
6
Click Save FAQ. The new entry appears in the table immediately.

Deactivating vs. Deleting an FAQ

ActionResultWhen to Use
Deactivate (set status to Inactive)Hidden from the public site but preserved in the databaseTemporarily remove a FAQ without losing the content (pending legal review, seasonal content)
DeletePermanently removed from the databaseThe question is entirely obsolete and will never be needed again

How to Reorder FAQs with Drag-and-Drop

1
Filter the FAQ table to show a single category using the Category dropdown.
2
Click and hold the drag handle (☰ icon) on the left side of any FAQ row and drag it to the desired position.
3
Click Save Order at the top of the table. The new display order is saved to the database.
⚠️
Save Order is a separate action

Reordering requires clicking the dedicated Save Order button — separate from the general Save button. If you close the FAQ Manager without clicking it, your reordering will be lost.

How to Export FAQs

1
Click Export FAQs at the top of the FAQ Manager.
2
Your browser downloads a JSON file containing all active FAQs — categories, questions, answers, and sort order. Use it as a backup or for content review outside the CMS.

Global Settings

Global Settings control site-wide values that appear across multiple pages. Click Global Settings in the sidebar to open it.

🚨
Changes are site-wide and immediate

When you save Global Settings, the changes take effect on every page of the website instantly. There is no preview mode. Double-check all values before clicking Save.

Available Global Settings Fields

FieldWhere It AppearsNotes
Site TitleBrowser tab titleTypically "CaltransBizConnect" — change only if the program is rebranded
Site DescriptionMeta description fallbackKeep under 160 characters; should describe the site's purpose clearly
Contact EmailFooter, contact page, notificationsUse the official Caltrans program email; changes here update all footers
Phone NumberFooter and contact pageFormat as (916) 324-1700 for consistency
Social Media LinksFooter social iconsFull URLs only; leave blank to hide that social icon
Logo URLSite header and footerMust be a URL to an image in the Media Library or an absolute external URL
Favicon URLBrowser tab iconSmall square image (32×32 or 16×16 pixels), preferably .ico or .png

How to Update Global Settings

1
Click Global Settings in the CMS sidebar. The settings form loads with all current values.
2
Update only the fields you need to change — leave others as-is.
3
Review your changes carefully, then click Save Settings. A success message confirms the save. Changes are live immediately.

Media Library

The Media Library is a centralized repository for all images used in CMS-editable sections. Click Media Library in the sidebar to open it. All uploaded images are shown as a grid of thumbnails.

How to Upload an Image

1
In the Media Library, locate the Upload Image section below the media grid.
2
Click Choose File (or drag and drop a file onto the upload area). Accepted formats: JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP, SVG. Maximum file size: 10 MB.
3
In the Alt Text field, type a description of the image. This is required by Caltrans accessibility policy. Write what the image shows — for example, "Construction workers installing a guardrail on a California highway" — not generic text like "image" or "photo."
4
Click Upload. The image is uploaded to the server and appears in the media grid immediately.
⚠️
Alt text is required — and matters

Screen reader users cannot see images; they rely entirely on alt text. Write descriptive, specific alt text for every image. Never use filenames as alt text.

Image Size Recommendations

Use CaseRecommended SizeFormat
Hero / Banner background1400 × 600 px minimumJPEG (smaller file size)
Card images600 × 400 pxJPEG or WebP
Logos / Icons200 × 200 px (square)PNG or SVG
Thumbnails400 × 300 pxJPEG or WebP
💡
Optimize before uploading

Large images slow down page load for all visitors. Compress your images using Squoosh or TinyPNG before uploading. Aim for under 300 KB for most images.

How to Select an Image When Editing a Section

1
While editing a section with a media-type field, click Choose Image. The Media Picker modal opens.
2
Click the image thumbnail you want to select. The modal closes and the image is applied to the section.
3
The image's existing alt text is filled in automatically. Override it if this specific usage needs a different description. Click Save.

How to Delete a Media File

1
In the Media Library grid, click the Delete button below the image thumbnail.
2
Confirm in the dialog. The file is permanently deleted from the server.
🚫
Check before deleting

If an image is currently used in a page section, deleting it from the Media Library will cause that section to display a broken image. Before deleting, find and replace any uses of that image in page sections first.

Changing Your Password

You can update your CMS login password directly within the CMS — no need to contact a developer or administrator.

How to Change Your CMS Password

1
Click Change Password in the CMS sidebar (near the bottom).
2
Enter your Current Password in the first field.
3
Enter your new password in the New Password field. Minimum 8 characters; mix uppercase and lowercase letters and include at least one number or special character.
4
Enter your new password again in the Confirm New Password field to catch any typos.
5
Click Update Password. A confirmation message appears. Your new password takes effect immediately and you'll need it at the next login.

Password Best Practices

  • Change your password every 90 days, or immediately if you suspect it has been compromised.
  • Never use a password you use on any other website or application.
  • Do not share your CMS password with colleagues — each admin should have their own account.
  • Consider using a password manager (such as 1Password or Bitwarden) to generate and store a strong, unique password.

Best Practices

Content Quality Standards

  • Write in plain language. Avoid jargon. If a 10th-grader can't understand your content, rewrite it. Caltrans accessibility standards require plain language across all public-facing content.
  • Be consistent. Use the same terminology throughout the site. Always write "Small Business Enterprise" on first reference, followed by "(SBE)" in parentheses.
  • Keep sentences short. Aim for 20 words or fewer per sentence in body copy.
  • Proofread before saving. Changes go live immediately. Read your content aloud before clicking Save.

Accessibility Requirements

  • Always provide alt text for images. Never leave the alt text field blank when uploading to the Media Library.
  • Don't use color alone to convey meaning. Make sure the meaning is also clear from the text itself.
  • Test your links. Every URL you add must be valid and working at the time you publish it.

Preview Before Publishing

1
After saving, click the View button (live page link) on the page in the Pages list.
2
Review the page in the live view on a desktop-sized window, then resize or use DevTools to check the mobile view.
3
If anything looks wrong, return to the CMS and make corrections.

Content Review Workflow

For significant content updates — especially program eligibility requirements, fee information, or legal language — follow this review process before publishing:

1
Draft the content in a shared Google Doc or Word document.
2
Have at least one colleague review the draft for accuracy and clarity.
3
Obtain approval from the program manager or legal team for content involving program rules or compliance language.
4
Only then enter the reviewed, approved content into the CMS and save.
✅
Good content update cadence

Review and refresh the FAQ and program overview pages at least quarterly. Outdated information — especially around deadlines, contact details, and program requirements — erodes user trust and can cause compliance issues.

Troubleshooting

My login is failing even though my password is correct

  • Confirm you are going to /admin-cms.html, not /login.html (which is the platform login for vendors and agencies).
  • Check for browser autofill filling in an old password — clear the field and type manually.
  • Your session may have been invalidated by a password change. Try logging in from a fresh browser tab.
  • If the problem persists, contact the platform administrator to reset your CMS credentials.

I saved a page but my changes are not showing on the live site

  • Hard-refresh the live page: press Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac) to bypass the browser cache.
  • Open the live page in a private/incognito window — this always loads fresh content.
  • If the old content still shows after a hard refresh, check that the save action completed successfully (look for the green "Saved" confirmation in the CMS).

I can't upload an image — the upload gives an error

  • Verify the file is one of the accepted formats: JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP, SVG.
  • Check the file size — it must be under 10 MB. Compress the image and try again.
  • Ensure you have filled in the Alt Text field — the upload will not proceed without it.

My FAQ reordering was not saved

After dragging FAQs to their new positions, you must click the dedicated Save Order button at the top of the FAQ table. This is a separate action from the general Save button.

I deleted a section or page by mistake

Deletions in the CMS are permanent and cannot be undone from within the tool. If the deletion was very recent, contact the server administrator immediately to request a database restore from backup. For small sections, recreate the content manually using the Add Section feature.
